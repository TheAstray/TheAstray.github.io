About the Album

A MOONLIGHTNIGHT

The album resembles drifting away into sleep and every song is a different dream. Its about sleeping at the edge of dreams and nightmares between melancolic romanticism, reflecting the past and the tales of the dark. With songs that capture the emotional depths of falling in love, trying to stand up after getting thrown to the ground and learning from lessons of the past.

  • Made of Tears

    - Blue at the Dreamers Ball -

    [...]
    You greet my love and my power fades
    My deeds were all betrayed
    Finding out the guilt
    Is it vain?
    [...]

    Made of Tears” is like a cry. A cry for our strongest desires: love, trust, and everlasting loyalty. This song describes not only a process, or the story of a romantic journey. It’s a chest keeping my emotions, my wishes, and desires inside. This chest was mostly shaped by the last few months, which dug me a seemingly bottomless pit, that I can’t escape, held back by the love I have lost. The song is drenched in dreary melancholy, my loyal companion constantly staying at my side while I’m drowning in past times. This song is like following the metaphorical invitation described in Queen’s “Dreamers Ball”: “If I can’t have you when I’m waking; I go to sleep and dream I’m with you” But this ball is like a prison where I’m waiting to be rescued. Waiting for true love to come for me, to heal my wounds and help me through the sinister night. It’s a cry of all the souls, doomed by love to sink into the depths of solence, hoping that the last light in the dark won’t die. We loved this Queen classic, but after everything that happened, I can’t stand listening to this song anymore. I never wanted us to end up dancing at the ball and I’m sorry that it had to end that way. Farewell, my dear Aries. Thank you for giving me the feeling of being loved by someone -M
